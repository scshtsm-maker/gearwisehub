Quick Verdict

Rating: 3.8 / 5 — Great sound and comfort, but ANC falls behind 2026 competition

Jabra Elite 85t on Amazon ↗

The Jabra Elite 85t was a strong contender when it launched, but 2026's landscape has moved on. Its semi-open design — once a refreshing alternative to pressure-sealed earbuds — now means its ANC simply can't keep up with fully sealed competitors like the AirPods Pro 2 or the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC. However, if you prioritize comfort and sound quality over maximum silence, and you can find it under $100, the 85t still has appeal.

The Semi-Open Design

The 85t's defining feature is its semi-open design. Small vents in each earbud allow air to circulate, reducing the "plugged ear" pressure that some people find uncomfortable with sealed earbuds. This design choice has real trade-offs:

  • Pro: No ear pressure — you can wear these for hours without that stuffed-up feeling
  • Pro: More natural sound — your own voice doesn't sound boomy during calls
  • Con: Less passive isolation — outside sound leaks in even without ANC
  • Con: ANC has to work harder to compensate, and can't match sealed designs

If you've ever found sealed earbuds uncomfortable or claustrophobic, the 85t's semi-open approach might be exactly what you need. But if you want maximum quiet, look elsewhere.

Noise Cancellation

The 85t uses dual microphones per earbud with Jabra's Advanced ANC. In 2026, it ranks as "good but not great." Low-frequency noise (HVAC, bus engines) is reduced by about 60-65% — noticeable but not immersive. Mid and high-frequency noise (voices, keyboard clicks) leaks through more than competitors.

Jabra's Sound+ app offers five ANC levels (1-5) plus a HearThrough mode. Level 5 provides the strongest cancellation but introduces a slight hiss. We found level 3-4 to be the sweet spot — enough noise reduction without the artifact.

Compared to competitors: The Soundcore Liberty 4 NC at $99 blocks noticeably more noise. The Sony WH-1000XM5 is in a different league entirely.

Sound Quality

This is where the 85t shines. The 12mm speakers deliver warm, detailed sound with excellent bass extension for an earbud. The semi-open design creates a wider soundstage than most sealed earbuds — music sounds more open and less "in your head."

Genre performance:

  • Pop/Hip-hop: Bass hits with authority without muddying the mids
  • Rock: Guitars have crunch and separation; drums are punchy
  • Classical: Wide soundstage benefits orchestral pieces; strings are detailed
  • Podcasts: Clear vocal reproduction; natural sound from the semi-open design

The Sound+ app includes a full 5-band EQ with presets. The "Bass Boost" and "Smooth" profiles are the most useful. You can also create custom profiles and save them.

Battery Life

The 85t delivers 5.5 hours with ANC on and 25 hours total with the case. In 2026, this is below average — the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC offers 10h/50h at half the price.

  • Single charge (ANC on): 5.5 hours
  • Single charge (ANC off): 7 hours
  • Total with case: 25 hours
  • Quick charge: 15 min = ~1 hour playback
  • Case charging: USB-C only (no wireless)

Comfort & Fit

Comfort is the 85t's strongest selling point. The semi-open design eliminates ear pressure, and at 7g per earbud, they're lightweight. The oval ear tips come in three sizes and create a gentle seal without the vacuum effect of fully sealed designs.

In our 6-hour comfort test, the 85t caused zero fatigue. This is unusual — most sealed earbuds start feeling uncomfortable after 3-4 hours. If you've had comfort issues with other earbuds, the 85t is worth trying for this reason alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Jabra Elite 85t still worth buying in 2026?

Only at a deep discount (under $100). At full price, the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC offers better ANC and battery for less. The 85t's strengths are comfort and sound quality, not noise cancellation.

What does semi-open design mean for ANC?

The 85t has small vents that let some outside sound in by design. This reduces ear pressure and creates a more natural sound, but it also means ANC can't block as much noise as fully sealed earbuds like the AirPods Pro 2.

Does Jabra Elite 85t support multipoint?

Yes. The 85t supports simultaneous connection to two devices via Bluetooth multipoint. You can be connected to your laptop and phone at the same time and switch audio sources automatically.

Jabra Elite 85t vs. Jabra Elite 10 — which should I buy?

The Elite 10 is the newer model with better ANC, longer battery, and spatial audio. It costs $200 vs. the 85t's $130 street price. If budget allows, the Elite 10 is the better earbud. But at under $100, the 85t is a solid value for comfort-focused listeners.
