Why Noise-Canceling Headphones Under $200?

You don't need to spend $350+ on Sony or Bose to get meaningful noise cancellation. The sub-$200 category has improved dramatically — brands like Anker, Edifier, and 1MORE now deliver ANC performance that was premium-tier just two years ago. If you commute, work in an open office, or just want peace at home, these headphones offer real value without compromise on the features that matter most.

For context, we also tested the Sony WH-1000XM5 ($348) as a reference point — our budget picks achieve 70-85% of its ANC effectiveness at less than half the price.

How We Test

Buying Tips

Over-Ear vs. Earbuds for ANC

Over-ear headphones create a physical seal around your ears — this passive isolation adds 10-15dB of noise reduction on top of active ANC. Earbuds rely entirely on the ANC chip and ear tip seal. If maximum quiet is your goal, over-ear wins. For portability and exercise, earbuds are the way to go.

Don't Overpay for Features You Won't Use

Most people use ANC in 1-2 scenarios. If you only need office noise reduction, you don't need adaptive ANC with 5 modes — a single-mode toggle works fine. Save the $50 and get the Edifier instead of over-ear options you won't fully utilize.

Check for Multipoint If You Switch Devices

Multipoint Bluetooth lets you connect to two devices simultaneously — laptop and phone, for example. If you take calls on your laptop and listen to music on your phone, this feature saves you from constant re-pairing. The Anker Space One, 1MORE SonoFlow, and Soundcore Liberty 4 NC all support it.

Are budget ANC headphones good for airplane travel?

Over-ear models like the Anker Soundcore Space One and 1MORE SonoFlow perform well on flights, reducing engine drone by 70-80%. For the best airplane ANC, over-ear designs generally outperform earbuds at any price point.

What is the best noise-canceling headphone under $200 overall?

Do budget ANC headphones have good microphones for calls?

Most models in this range have acceptable — not great — call quality. The Anker Space One and Soundcore Liberty 4 NC are the best of the bunch, with dedicated call modes that reduce background noise. For serious video conferencing, consider a standalone mic.
